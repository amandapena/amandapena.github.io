Welcome to my home on the internet. Thanks for stopping by.

I'm a digital campaigning professional based in Boston, MA. Currently, I work at Oxfam America, leading the way on digital campaigning and managing our social media platforms. My job is pretty cool because I get to work across all of our campaigning issues, from our highly successful #RefugeesWelcome campaign to build public support for refugees, to our massive push to #FightFamine in four countries on the brink of famine.

I'm also Chair of our Diversity and Inclusion Working Group, and was recently elected by my peers to be a co-chair of our union.

I've been working in digital engagement for four years. Before I worked at Oxfam, I was a Senior Associate at M+R where I worked for a bunch of amazing clients, including Planned Parenthood, Union of Concerned Scientists, Bread for the World, and Pathfinder International, on digital fundraising and advocacy.

I got my start in the campaigning world at age 14 when I first began interning for then State Rep. Jamie Eldridge – and I couldn't have asked for a better mentor (thanks Jamie!). Ever since then, I've had a fire in my belly for organizing and activating people to make change. And I'm obsessed with how the internet can be a tool for social change.

Outside of work, I also help produce the Do Your Homework Podcast, a new podcast by three young hip-hop heads in Boston who discuss current events in hip-hop and all the amazing history that brought us to this moment.
